Careers

The graphic design career is based on the knowledge of creating visual concepts, using computer software or by hand, to communicate ideas that inspire, inform and captivate. Graphic design is the profession and academic discipline whose activity consists of projecting visual communications aimed at transmitting specific messages to social groups with specific objectives.

Interior Design is a project discipline that involves the process of improving the function and qualities of the interior space, with the manipulation of the spatial volume, as well as the surface treatment (materials and objects). Interior design students study the artistic, creative, and technical principles involved in creating attractive interior spaces. Interior design specialists can pursue careers in interior design, textiles and furniture, as well as architectural technology and property management.

The Visual Arts career is a training and learning space that develops skills to apply the fundamental concepts, practices and procedures in the visual arts, from an integral perspective that contributes to contemporary artistic production. The objectives for our students in Visual Arts are that they be able to analyze processes of production of work and construction of theoretical and historical discourses in the visual arts. Visual arts include art forms such as drawing, painting, sculpture, crafts, printmaking, and photography.

Financial Assistance

  • Work-Study programs: Undergraduate and graduate students with work-study jobs will work part-time on or off campus while enrolled.
  • Loans:You must repay your loan, so be sure you understand your options and responsibilities.
  • Scholarships:Start researching early, and meet deadlines, and you may be on your way to scholarship success.
  • Grants:The federal government provides grants for students attending college or career school. Most types of grants, unlike loans, are sources of free money that generally do not have to be repaid.

Scholarships

Our university count with two types of scholarships:
William A. Bouguereau scholarship
These scholarships with no requirements usually have many applicants because it takes very little time to actually complete the application. These types of scholarships do not take into account academic performance, financial need or extracurricular involvement. 

Berthe Morisot scholarship
These scholarships are awarded for things like citizenship status, average grade or economic situation. The requirements for these types of scholarships vary greatly.
