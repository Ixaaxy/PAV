Financial Assistance

  • Work-Study programs: Undergraduate and graduate students with work-study jobs will work part-time on or off campus while enrolled.
  • Loans:You must repay your loan, so be sure you understand your options and responsibilities.
  • Scholarships:Start researching early, and meet deadlines, and you may be on your way to scholarship success.
  • Grants:The federal government provides grants for students attending college or career school. Most types of grants, unlike loans, are sources of free money that generally do not have to be repaid.

Scholarships

Our university count with two types of scholarships:
William A. Bouguereau scholarship
These scholarships with no requirements usually have many applicants because it takes very little time to actually complete the application. These types of scholarships do not take into account academic performance, financial need or extracurricular involvement. 

Berthe Morisot scholarship
These scholarships are awarded for things like citizenship status, average grade or economic situation. The requirements for these types of scholarships vary greatly.

Admission Requirements

  • Have English proficiency
  • As for the grade point average, if there is a minimum; it will be 4.0, counting the 3 years prior to the application.
